Foie Gras

A luxury food made from the liver of fattened ducks or geese, foie gras is prized for its rich, buttery texture and delicate flavor. It is often served as a pâté, terrine, or as an accompaniment to dishes like steak or salads.




Escargots de Bourgogne

A classic French dish consisting of snails cooked in garlic butter, parsley, and sometimes wine. Escargots are typically served as an appetizer, often with crusty bread to soak up the flavorful sauce.





Coq au Vin

A traditional French dish featuring chicken braised in red wine with mushrooms, onions, bacon, and herbs. Coq au Vin is known for its robust flavors and tender meat, and it is often served with mashed potatoes or crusty bread.



Bouillabaisse

A seafood stew originating from the port city of Marseille, bouillabaisse is made with a variety of fish and shellfish, flavored with saffron, tomatoes, garlic, and herbs. It is served with rouille, a spicy garlic mayonnaise, and crusty bread.


.

Macarons

Delicate, colorful almond meringue cookies sandwiched together with a creamy filling, macarons are a beloved French sweet treat. They come in a variety of flavors, including pistachio, raspberry, chocolate, and lemon.





Croissants

Iconic French pastries made from buttery, flaky dough, croissants are enjoyed throughout France and around the world. They are often eaten for breakfast or as a snack, plain or filled with chocolate, almond paste, or ham and cheese.





Truffles

Highly prized and often referred to as "black diamonds," truffles are aromatic fungi that grow underground. In French cuisine, black truffles from Périgord and white truffles from Alba are particularly esteemed and used to flavor dishes like omelets, pasta, and risottos.




Cassoulet

A hearty casserole originating from the south of France, cassoulet is made with white beans, various meats such as duck, pork, and sausage, and flavored with tomatoes, garlic, and herbs. It is slow-cooked until the flavors meld together, resulting in a comforting and satisfying dish.

Crepes

Thin pancakes made from a simple batter of flour, eggs, milk, and butter, crepes are a versatile French delicacy enjoyed in both sweet and savory variations. They can be filled with ingredients like Nutella, jam, cheese, ham, or mushrooms, making them suitable for breakfast, dessert, or a light meal.
