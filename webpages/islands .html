Corsica

Located in the Mediterranean Sea, Corsica is the fourth-largest island in the Mediterranean and one of France's 18 regions. It is renowned for its rugged landscapes, pristine beaches, and picturesque coastal towns. Corsica offers opportunities for hiking, water sports, and exploring historic sites such as the ancient city of Bonifacio and the Calanques de Piana.

Île de Ré

Situated off the west coast of France near La Rochelle, Île de Ré is a popular holiday destination known for its sandy beaches, charming villages, and salt marshes. Visitors can enjoy cycling along the island's network of bike paths, sampling fresh seafood, and exploring historic sites like the Vauban Fortifications.

Île de Porquerolles

Part of the Îles d'Hyères archipelago in the Mediterranean, Île de Porquerolles is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and protected natural areas. Visitors can explore the island on foot or by bike, visit the Fort Sainte Agathe, and relax on picturesque beaches like Plage Notre Dame.

Île d'Oléron

Situated off the Atlantic coast near La Rochelle, Île d'Oléron is France's second-largest island after Corsica. It is known for its oyster farming, sandy beaches, and charming seaside villages. Visitors can enjoy water sports, cycling along scenic trails, and visiting attractions such as the Château d'Oléron and the Phare de Chassiron lighthouse.

Île de Noirmoutier

Connected to the mainland by a bridge and a tidal causeway, Île de Noirmoutier is famous for its salt marshes, sandy beaches, and traditional fishing villages. Visitors can explore historic sites like the Château de Noirmoutier, relax on beaches like Plage des Dames, and sample local delicacies such as salted butter caramel.
