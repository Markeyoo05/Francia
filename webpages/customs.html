Greetings

In France, it is customary to greet with a handshake, often accompanied by "Bonjour" (Good morning) or "Bonsoir" (Good evening). Cheek kissing (la bise) is also common, especially among friends and family, with two or three kisses on alternating cheeks depending on the region.

Mealtime

Dining is an essential aspect of French customs. Meals are typically leisurely affairs, often starting with an apéritif (pre-dinner drink) followed by multiple courses. Dinner is generally served later in the evening compared to some other cultures.


Wine and Cheese

France is famous for its wine and cheese, and both are deeply ingrained in French culture. Wine is often enjoyed with meals, and there is a wide variety of cheeses, each with its own regional specialties and traditions.



Fashion and Style

French fashion is renowned worldwide for its elegance and sophistication. Paris, in particular, is considered a global fashion capital, hosting renowned fashion events such as Paris Fashion Week.




Art and Literature

France has a rich artistic and literary tradition, with celebrated figures such as Claude Monet, Vincent van Gogh, Victor Hugo, and Marcel Proust. Art galleries, museums, and literary cafés are integral parts of French cultural life.



Festivals and Celebrations

France hosts numerous festivals and celebrations throughout the year, including Bastille Day (July 14th), which commemorates the French Revolution, and Mardi Gras (Fat Tuesday), a festive pre-Lenten celebration marked by parades and feasting.

Education and Intellectualism

France has a long-standing tradition of valuing education and intellectual pursuits. The French take pride in their language and literature, and there is a strong emphasis on critical thinking and debate.



Laïcité

France has a concept of laïcité, which refers to the strict secularism enshrined in its laws. This principle separates religion from the state and ensures religious freedom while maintaining the neutrality of public institutions.
